# Plurals

Dutch pluralization follows relatively predictable rules compared to English.

While English has many irregular plurals (like "child" → "children" or "mouse" → "mice"), Dutch primarily uses a small set of suffixes and modifications.

### Rule 1: -en (Most Common)

The default plural suffix in Dutch is **-en**.

| Singular | Plural | Notes |
|----------|--------|-------|
| de tas (the bag) | de tas**sen** | Standard -en addition |
| de boom (the tree) | de boom**en** | No spelling change |
| het boek (the book) | de boek**en** | "het" becomes "de" in plural |

**Examples:**

- de vrouw (the woman) → de vrouw**en** (the women)
- de auto (the car) → de auto**s** → Actually, this follows another rule! (see below)

### Rule 2: -s

Words ending in certain sounds take **-s** instead of -en.

**Apply -s to words ending in:**

- Vowels (a, e, i, o, u): auto → auto**'s**
- Diphthongs ending in -el, -er, -en: spiegel → spiegel**s**
- -pr, -tr, -cl, -cr, -gr: patroon → patroon**s**

**Examples:**

- de auto (the car) → de auto**'s**
- de koffie (the coffee) → de koffie**s**
- het hotel (the hotel) → de hotel**s**

### Rule 3: No Change (Zero Plural)

Some words don't change at all in the plural.

| Singular | Plural |
|----------|--------|
| de auto | de auto ('s also works) |
| het hotel | de hotel ('s also works) |

This is more common in formal or older Dutch, and many speakers add -s anyway.

### Rule 4: Irregular Plurals

A small group of words have unique plural forms.

| Singular | Plural |
|----------|--------|
| het kind (the child) | de kind**eren** |
| de man (the man) | de man**nen** |
| de vrouw (the woman) | de vrouw**en** |
| het been (the leg) | de benen |
| het blad (the leaf) | de blad**eren** |
| de daad (the deed) | de daden |

---

## Vowel Changes (Stein/Voorwaarts)

Some words undergo a vowel change (umlaut) when pluralized.

This is similar to how English changes "foot" to "feet" or "mouse" to "mice."

**Common patterns:**

| Singular | Plural | Vowel Change |
|----------|--------|--------------|
| de stad (the city) | de sted**en** | a → e |
| het land (the country) | de land**en** | No umlaut, just -en |
| de man (the man) | de man**nen** | a → a (double n) |
| de kaas (the cheese) | de kaz**en** | aa → a (shortened) |

---

## Special Cases

### Diminutives

Dutch diminutives (words ending in -je, -pje, -etje, etc.) form their plurals with -s:

- het meisje (the girl) → de meisje**s**
- het kindje (the little child) → de kindje**s**

### Words Ending in -ie

Words with -ie typically add **-s** or **-ën**:

- de foto (the photo) → de foto**'s**
- de economie → de economie**ën**

### Foreign Words

Borrowed words often follow the language of origin:

| Original | Dutch Plural |
|----------|--------------|
| het museum | de museum**s** (or museum**en**) |
| de foto | de foto**'s** |
| het idee | de idee**ën** |

### Compound Words

Pluralize the main (last) component:

- de tuinman (the gardener) → de tuinmannen
- de kinderkamer (the children's room) → de kinderkamers
